## Questions (5 templates)

### PH-6: "How does scoring work?"
> Great question, [Name]! We evaluate prompts across 6 dimensions using Claude as the AI backbone:
> - **P**recision — How specific is the request?
> - **R**ole — Is there a clear persona/expertise?
> - **O**utput — Is the expected format defined?
> - **M**odifiers — Are there constraints and parameters?
> - **S**pecificity — How detailed is the context?
> - **T**esting — Can the output be verified?
>
> Each gets a 0-100 score, then weighted into an overall grade (D through S-tier). Try it — the feedback is quite detailed!

### PH-7: "Is this free?"
> Yep! Core grading is completely free — score unlimited prompts, get detailed feedback on all 6 dimensions, and share your results. We have a Pro tier coming soon for power users (bulk analysis, export, rewrite suggestions) but the core tool will always be free.

### PH-8: "What AI model do you use?"
> We use Claude (Anthropic) for the evaluation engine. We chose it for its strong reasoning and nuanced feedback capability. The scoring rubric is carefully calibrated across thousands of prompt evaluations to ensure consistency. Each dimension has specific criteria that map to real-world prompt effectiveness.

### PH-9: "Privacy — do you store prompts?"
> Privacy is a priority for us, [Name]. We don't store the actual prompt text on our servers after evaluation. Results are stored in your browser's session storage. If you choose to share a score card, only the scores and metadata are saved — never the prompt itself. You can check our approach at scoremyprompt.com.

### PH-10: "Can I use this for [specific use case]?"
> Absolutely, [Name]! ScoreMyPrompt works with any type of prompt — coding, marketing, creative writing, data analysis, you name it. You can select your job role before scoring, and the evaluation adjusts its rubric accordingly. Try it with your [specific use case] prompts and see how they score!
